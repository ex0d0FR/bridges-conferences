Desktop Automation

Using Cowork

Cowork is Anthropic's desktop tool that lets Claude operate your computer directly, opening files, navigating websites, filling forms, and running automations on your behalf.

What is Cowork? A beta desktop app by Anthropic that connects Claude to your local computer. Claude can see your screen, click buttons, open apps, and move files, like having an AI assistant physically at your desk.
1
Install Cowork on your computer
Download the Cowork desktop app from Anthropic. Works on Mac and Windows. Sign in with your Claude account.
anthropic.com/cowork
2
Save this HTML file locally
Download conference_ai_template.html to a folder on your computer. This is the master template Cowork will read, edit and manage.
/Documents/BMB-Conference/
conference_ai_template.html
city-config-milan.json
3
Create a city config JSON file
Ask Cowork to create a city-config-milan.json with all Milan variables. Claude writes and saves it for you.
{ "city": "Milan",
"year": 2026,
"dates": "Oct 27-30",
"luma_url": "lu.ma/milan2026" }
4
Give natural language instructions
Open Cowork and type what you need. Claude reads your files, browses the web, and updates documents without you doing it manually.
"Set up Milan 2026. Read config,
find top 5 venues 800+ capacity,
add them to the template."
5
Automate the robot checklist tasks
Tell Cowork to handle the robot tasks. Claude opens your browser, goes to Luma, configures tickets, then sets up the MailerLite email sequence, all on its own.
"Open lu.ma, create event Milan 2026,
add 3 ticket tiers from config,
set early bird deadline Aug 31."
6
Keep template updated year over year
After each conference tell Cowork: Close Paris 2025 and start Milan 2026. Claude archives old files, updates the template with learnings, and begins the next city cycle.
